Resources

Resources of the Department

The Department of Computer Science has various resources that benefit its users and students, and prepare them for the tech industry. Some of these resources are:

  • Computer Labs: The department has four computer labs, equipped with modern computers, software, and internet access. The computer labs are used for teaching, learning, and research purposes, as well as for conducting exams, workshops, and competitions. The computer labs are open to all students and staff of the department, and can be booked for special events. The computer labs help the users and students to acquire practical skills and experience in various aspects of computer science and technology.
  • Library: The department has a library, which contains books, journals, magazines, and other materials related to computer science and technology. The library also provides access to online databases, e-books, and e-journals, as well as computers and printers. The library is open to all students and staff of the department, and can be used for studying, researching, and borrowing materials.The library helps the users and students to enhance their knowledge and understanding of various topics and trends in computer science and technology.
  • Online Short Courses: The department offers online short courses, which are intensive eight-week professional certificate courses in various topics, such as web programming, information security, computer networks, and mobile application development. The courses are open to senior high school graduates, tertiary students, graduates, workers, and the general public. The courses are held online, and participants receive certificates upon completion. The courses help the users and students to learn new skills and technologies, and to improve their employability and career prospects.

Resources of the School

The Department of Computer Science is under the School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences, which is one of the five schools under the College of Basic and Applied Sciences. The School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences also has various resources that benefit its users and students, and prepare them for the tech industry. Some of these resources are:

  • Supercomputer: The school has a supercomputer, which is a high-performance computing system that can perform complex and large-scale calculations and simulations. The supercomputer is used for research and innovation purposes, as well as for teaching and learning purposes. The supercomputer is open to all students and staff of the school, and can be accessed remotely or on-site. The supercomputer helps the users and students to conduct advanced and cutting-edge research and innovation in various fields of physical and mathematical sciences.
  • Laboratories: The school has various laboratories, which are specialized facilities that contain equipment, instruments, and materials for conducting experiments and tests. The laboratories are used for research and innovation purposes, as well as for teaching and learning purposes. The laboratories are open to all students and staff of the school, and can be booked for special events. The laboratories help the users and students to acquire practical skills and experience in various aspects of physical and mathematical sciences.
  • Research Week: The school hosts a biennial research week, where students and staff from the six departments of the school present and showcase their research projects, publications, and innovations, and receive feedback and recognition from their peers, mentors, and experts. The research week also features keynote speeches, plenary sessions, and poster sessions, on various themes and topics related to the fields of physical and mathematical sciences. The research week is open to all members of the school, as well as other interested individuals. The research week helps the users and students to foster a culture of research excellence, collaboration, and innovation among the school's students and staff, as well as to enhance the visibility and impact of the school's research.
